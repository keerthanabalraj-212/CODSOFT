APJ ABDUL KALAM

1931-2015

"Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action."

Biography

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (15 October 1931 – 27 July 2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007. Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he studied physics and aerospace engineering and spent four decades as a scientist at DRDO and ISRO. Known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology, he played a key role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998. Elected president with support from both major political parties, he was called the "People's President." After his term, he focused on education, writing, and public service. He received the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor. Kalam died on 27 July 2015 while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong. His funeral in Rameswaram was attended by thousands and he was buried with full state honors.

Early Life and Education

Family Background: Kalam was born into a Tamil Muslim family. His father, Jainulabdeen, was a boat owner and imam of a local mosque, and his mother, Ashiamma, was a housewife. Schooling: Kalam attended Schwartz Higher Secondary School in Ramanathapuram. Higher Education: He graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and later studied Aerospace Engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT).

Career and Contributions

INDIAN SPACE RESEARCH ORGANISATION (ISRO)

Joined ISRO: In 1969, Kalam joined ISRO, where he was the project director of India’s first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III), which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit in July 1980. Key Projects: Worked on the development of the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and the SLV-III.

DEFENCE RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT ORGANISATION (DRDO)

Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP): Kalam led the IGMDP, which resulted in the development of missiles such as Agni, Prithvi, Akash, Trishul, and Nag. Role as Scientific Adviser: Served as the Scientific Adviser to the Defence Minister and later as the Principal Scientific Adviser to the Government of India.

PRESIDENCY (2002-2007)

11th President of India: Kalam served as the President of India from July 25, 2002, to July 25, 2007. His presidency was marked by a focus on development, particularly in the fields of science and technology, and he became popularly known as the "People’s President."

Literary Works

APJ Abdul Kalam was a prolific writer whose works reflect his vision for India's development and his deep belief in the power of education and technology. His notable books include: India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium - Co-authored with Yagnaswami Sundara Rajan, this book outlines a comprehensive plan to transform India into a developed nation by 2020. It highlights five core areas: agriculture, education and healthcare, information technology, infrastructure, and self-reliance in critical technologies.

Wings of Fire

An autobiography that traces Kalam's journey from his humble beginnings in Rameswaram to becoming a leading aerospace scientist and the President of India. The book is an inspiring account of his life, challenges, and achievements.

Ignited Minds

Unleashing the Power Within India - This book emphasizes the importance of education and the role of young people in shaping the future of India. Kalam shares his experiences and ideas to motivate the youth to dream big and work hard to achieve their goals.

Transcendence

My Spiritual Experiences with Pramukh Swamiji - Published shortly before his death, this book delves into Kalam's spiritual journey and his relationship with Pramukh Swami, the spiritual leader of the BAPS Swaminarayan Sanstha. It emphasizes the role of spirituality in achieving personal and national development.
Kalam's writings continue to inspire millions with their emphasis on hard work, innovation, and the potential of India's youth to transform the nation.
